Frequently Asked Questions

Is GPS tracking ethical? How will my child react? +
Yes, when used responsibly. Many parents find tracking helpful for safety, especially for younger children. We recommend transparency—tell your child they're wearing a safety device. As they grow older, discuss boundaries. Tracking shows care, not control.
How accurate is the GPS location? +
Outdoors: 3-5 meters accuracy using GPS satellites. Indoors: 20-50 meters using cell towers and WiFi. Urban areas have better accuracy than rural. Every location update includes coordinates, address, and map link for verification.
Can my child remove or hide the button? +
The button is small and can fit in a pocket or bag. You set up "tamper alerts"—if the button moves unexpectedly or goes offline, you receive an instant alert. We recommend teaching children why wearing it matters for their safety.
What if my child is in danger—how fast are alerts? +
If the child presses the panic button, alerts reach you within 2-3 seconds via SMS and app. Voice calls connect immediately if enabled. Location data is sent simultaneously. In Guardian+ plans, emergency services can be auto-alerted.
Do I need a smartphone for parent tracking? +
No. You can receive location updates via SMS on any phone. The app provides richer features (maps, history, zones), but SMS-only mode works on basic phones. Both are included in your plan.
What if my child goes to another country? +
Mlinzi Kids tracks globally. International roaming is included. The button works in 150+ countries. You'll get location updates from anywhere with cell signal. Perfect for family travels.
